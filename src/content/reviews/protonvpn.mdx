Proton VPN enters the market with a compelling proposition — privacy-first design from the creators of Proton Mail, backed by Swiss law. The service earns high marks for transparency and genuine commitment to user privacy.

## Speed & Performance
Proton VPN's WireGuard implementation delivers solid throughput — adequate for streaming, browsing, and most everyday use cases, though it trails the speed leaders (NordVPN, ExpressVPN) in head-to-head comparisons. OpenVPN UDP performance is acceptable but noticeably slower. Latency on nearby servers is reasonable; intercontinental connections add expected overhead. These speeds are fine for streaming and browsing but may be noticeable in competitive gaming.

## Streaming
Proton VPN's streaming support has improved significantly. On the Plus plan, Netflix, Amazon Prime Video, Disney+, and BBC iPlayer all work reliably. Max and Hulu can be less consistent. The free tier does not include streaming-optimized servers, so users who need streaming should plan on a paid subscription.

## The Free Tier
Unlike fake free tiers that throttle to unusable speeds or impose strict data caps, Proton VPN's free plan is genuinely functional — unlimited data, no logs, and servers in five countries. Speeds are capped at medium on the free tier, but there are no data limits and no ads. It's the best free VPN option available for basic privacy needs.

## Verdict
Proton VPN is the best choice for users who want a trustworthy free tier or who prioritize Swiss privacy protections. Paid plans are competitive but don't quite match the speed and streaming performance of NordVPN or ExpressVPN. For privacy-first users who also want streaming, the Plus plan is a solid option.
