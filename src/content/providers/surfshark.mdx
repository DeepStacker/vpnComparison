Launched in 2018, Surfshark quickly established itself as a feature-rich yet affordable VPN. The Netherlands headquarters places the company under Dutch law, which has data retention obligations — though Surfshark maintains a strict no-logs policy verified annually by Deloitte.

Surfshark's network of 3,200+ servers covers 100 countries. A standout feature is unlimited simultaneous connections — one subscription covers every device in your household. The service offers CleanWeb (ad/tracker/malware blocking), GPS spoofing on Android, MultiHop (double VPN), NoBorders mode for restrictive regions, and camouflage mode that disguises VPN traffic as regular traffic.

WireGuard, OpenVPN, and IKEv2 protocols are supported. Surfshark also provides a dedicated IP add-on and static IP options. The service works reliably with Netflix, Amazon Prime Video, Disney+, and BBC iPlayer.

Native apps are available for Windows, macOS, Linux, Android, iOS, Android TV, Fire TV, and routers. Browser extensions are available for Chrome, Firefox, and Edge. Surfshark was acquired by Nord Security (NordVPN's parent company) in 2022 but continues to operate independently.
