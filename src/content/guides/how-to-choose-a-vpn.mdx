## 1. Privacy Policy & Logging

The most important factor. A no-logs policy means the VPN provider does not store records of your online activity. Look for services that have had their no-logs policy independently audited.

## 2. Jurisdiction

Where the VPN company is headquartered determines which laws apply to their data handling. Jurisdictions outside the 14 Eyes intelligence alliance (Panama, British Virgin Islands, Switzerland) offer stronger privacy protections.

## 3. Security & Encryption

All reputable VPNs use AES-256 encryption and support modern protocols. Look for WireGuard support for the best balance of speed and security.

## 4. Server Network

A larger server network means more options for finding a fast, nearby server. It also provides more IP addresses for accessing geo-restricted content.

## 5. Speed

VPN overhead will slow your connection. Premium VPNs with modern protocols lose 10-20% of your base speed. Budget VPNs may lose 40-60%.

## 6. Streaming & Torrenting

Not all VPNs support streaming or P2P file sharing. Verify that your chosen VPN allows the activities you need.

## 7. Price & Value

The most expensive VPN isn't always the best, and the cheapest may compromise on privacy. Look for plans with money-back guarantees so you can test risk-free.
