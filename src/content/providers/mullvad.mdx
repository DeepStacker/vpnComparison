Mullvad has been operating since 2009 and is widely respected in the privacy community for its unwavering commitment to anonymity. Based in Sweden, the company operates under some of the strongest privacy protections in Europe, though Sweden is a member of the 14 Eyes alliance. Mullvad's transparent operations and consistent audit history mitigate jurisdictional concerns.

The service operates 700+ servers across 50 countries. Rather than chasing server count, Mullvad focuses on quality: all servers run on 10 Gbps connections and support the WireGuard protocol exclusively (OpenVPN support has been deprecated in favor of WireGuard-only to reduce attack surface). The company owns its own AS numbers and IP ranges, reducing reliance on third-party infrastructure.

Mullvad charges a flat €5/month regardless of subscription duration — no discounts for long-term plans, no upsells, no gimmicks. Payment options include credit card, PayPal, Bitcoin, Bitcoin Cash, Monero, bank transfer, and even cash sent by mail (instructions are provided on the website).

The service supports port forwarding via ephemeral ports and P2P traffic on all servers. Mullvad explicitly states it is not designed for bypassing geo-restrictions — it is a privacy tool, not a streaming unblocker.

Mullvad has undergone extensive independent auditing, including a complete no-logs policy audit by Assured AB and infrastructure penetration testing by Cure53.
