## Privacy & Anonymity

Both services excel in privacy. Mullvad's anonymous payment options (including cash) and Sweden base give it an edge for anonymity-seeking users. Proton VPN's Swiss jurisdiction and open-source transparency are strong arguments. Both have independently verified no-logs policies.

## Speed

Mullvad is faster — 850 Mbps vs Proton VPN's 480 Mbps on WireGuard. Mullvad's lower user density per server means more consistent speeds during peak hours.

## Streaming

Proton VPN offers limited but functional streaming support (Prime Video, BBC iPlayer). Mullvad's streaming support is essentially non-existent by design.

## Features

Proton VPN offers more features — NetShield ad blocking, Secure Core multi-hop, port forwarding, and a free tier. Mullvad is minimalist by design, offering only core VPN functionality.

## Pricing

Mullvad's flat €5/month is simple and fair. Proton VPN's free tier is unmatched for cost-conscious privacy seekers.

## Verdict

Proton VPN wins for most users thanks to its balance of privacy, features, and streaming capability. Choose Mullvad if your priority is maximum anonymity and you don't need streaming or advanced features.
