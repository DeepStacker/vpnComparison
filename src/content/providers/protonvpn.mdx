Proton VPN is developed by Proton AG, the Swiss company behind Proton Mail, the world's largest encrypted email service. Swiss privacy laws provide some of the strongest data protection globally. Proton VPN benefits from the company's broader mission of building a privacy-first internet ecosystem.

The server network includes 2,900+ servers across 69 countries, all physically owned and operated by Proton — no third-party or virtual servers. The service supports WireGuard, OpenVPN, and IKEv2 protocols, with a focus on strong encryption (AES-256) and Secure Core architecture that routes traffic through multiple servers in privacy-friendly countries.

The free tier is genuinely useful — no data cap, no ads, no logs — with servers in the US, Netherlands, and Japan, though speeds are intentionally limited to 2 Mbps and only one device is allowed. Premium plans unlock the full network, up to 10 devices, high-speed streaming, and features like NetShield ad blocking, port forwarding, and Tor over VPN.

Independent security audits by Securitum and SEC Consult have verified Proton VPN's no-logs claims and application security. The company also publishes its transparency reports and source code for community review.
