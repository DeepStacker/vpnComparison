ExpressVPN has been a premium player since 2009, building a reputation for reliability that few competitors match. Its proprietary Lightway protocol and TrustedServer technology represent genuine innovation in the VPN space.

## Speed & Performance
ExpressVPN's Lightway protocol is exceptionally fast — consistently among the top performers in every round of testing we've conducted. On gigabit connections, Lightway retains 90%+ of baseline throughput, making it effectively transparent for most use cases. Connection times are near-instant, typically completing before you notice the handshake. OpenVPN UDP performance is also strong, competitive with many providers' WireGuard implementations.

## Streaming
ExpressVPN is the most reliable streaming VPN we've tested. Netflix, Amazon Prime Video, Disney+, HBO Max, Hulu, and BBC iPlayer all worked without interruption throughout testing. The included MediaStreamer DNS is a genuine differentiator for users with game consoles, smart TVs, and Apple TV.

## Privacy
ExpressVPN's TrustedServer technology ensures all servers run on RAM, with no data written to hard drives — every reboot returns the server to a known-clean state. The company's no-logs policy has been verified by PwC, and the Lightway protocol underwent independent security review by Cure53. Additional audits by KPMG have validated the TrustedServer architecture.

It's worth noting that ExpressVPN was acquired by Kape Technologies in 2021. Kape's history in the ad-tech space has raised eyebrows in the privacy community, though ExpressVPN operates independently and its no-logs claims remain independently verified.

## Verdict
ExpressVPN remains the speed and streaming champion. If those are your top priorities and you're willing to pay a premium, it's hard to beat. Privacy-conscious users should weigh the Kape ownership question, but the technical and audit evidence supports ExpressVPN's no-logs claims.
