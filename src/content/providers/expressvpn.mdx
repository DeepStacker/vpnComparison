ExpressVPN has been a mainstay of the VPN industry since 2009. The company is based in the British Virgin Islands, a jurisdiction with favorable privacy laws outside the 14 Eyes intelligence-sharing alliance. With 3,000+ servers across 105 countries, ExpressVPN maintains one of the most geographically diverse networks in the industry.

ExpressVPN developed Lightway, its own open-source protocol built on WolfSSL that delivers faster connection times and better reliability than traditional protocols. The service also supports OpenVPN UDP/TCP, IKEv2, and L2TP/IPsec.

TrustedServer technology ensures every server runs on RAM only — no data persists after a reboot. The company has submitted to independent audits by PwC (no-logs policy verification), Cure53 (Lightway security audit), and KPMG (privacy compliance).

Every ExpressVPN subscription includes a full-featured MediaStreamer DNS service, which enables smart DNS-based unblocking on devices that don't support VPN configurations directly (game consoles, smart TVs, Apple TV).

ExpressVPN provides native apps for Windows, macOS, Linux, Android, iOS, Android TV, Fire TV, and routers. A browser extension is available for Chrome, Firefox, and Edge. Up to 8 simultaneous connections are allowed per subscription.
